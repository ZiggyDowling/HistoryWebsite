New Zealand Culture

The Maoris had a deep and rich culture that has existed for 1200 - 1300 years and this culture is still present in some place today. The Maoris were the first nations people of New Zealand. Traditonaly the Maoris relied of a social heirachy to run there towns. At the top was the ariki or supreme cheif who was the leader. Next was the rangatira or cheif. After the rangatira was the kaumatua or the elders followed by the tohunga or priest. Next was the tutua or commoners and at the very bottom were the taurekareka who were slaves. Each of these groups had diffrent roles and resposablities. Another big part of the Maori culture were art and carvings. These would be made by people who had spend years mastering this craft. These paintings ad carvings could have lots of diffrent meaning behind them and could sometimes tell a story. The maoris also had a wide range of food that they would make. The maoris had dishes with indgredients that came from the bush to the sea. A popular dish made from plants was called Pikopiko. This was made from fern shoots. Another dish called Tio was made with oysters. These are only some of the wide range of dishes that the maoris made. Finnaly one of the most well know parts of the Maori culture was called the Haka. This was a dance peforemed traditional a battle. This was done to intimidate the enemy. This is done today most commonly by the New Zealand rugby team, the all blacks.

This is so the other parts of the culture are not lost to time. The people of Easter Island would have massive wooden tablets that would contain writting. But no one knows what these scripts said because the charicter were forgoten and no one will ever know what they say. One of the most well know parts of the Easter Island culture and the Moai. These were the massive stone statues that were placed all across the island. No one knows how they got there or why they were put there. But people have made guesses. Another part of their culture was a competion was called the birdman contest. This was a contest that was done by two young men on Easter Island. They would have to climb down a 300 meter cliff, swim across the water to a small island and collect an egg. This would be a 1.5 kilometer swim. Lastly the people of Easter Island ate a diet consisting of sea birds, plants and fish. These were all caugh and collected.
